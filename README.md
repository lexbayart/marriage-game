## вҡ пёҸ A note before you play

This game is built in the **survival** genre вҖ” and it is deliberately hard.

It is not intended to offend anyone. The author's goal was to explore the real pressures of marriage through game mechanics, the same way other survival games explore starvation, war, or natural disaster. If something in this game makes you uncomfortable, that reaction is probably the point.

---

## рҹ’Ў Why this game exists

The author loves survival games вҖ” the kind where you keep coming back to something impossibly difficult. At some point, he asked himself: **what is the hardest thing people still voluntarily choose, over and over again?**

Marriage.

And within marriage вҖ” being the **wife**.

The author's observation is that society places the heaviest burden of a marriage's success or failure on the woman. She is more often blamed when it falls apart. The emotional labor, the children, the household вҖ” these land disproportionately on her. Meanwhile, the husband is often perceived as someone who simply lives his life.

So in this game, **you play as the wife.** The husband is an NPC вҖ” passive, absorbed in his own affairs. He wanders, works, rests on the couch. But you can set his direction. You determine where he goes and what he opens up. It's an old idea, expressed as a game mechanic: the husband is the head, the wife is the neck вҖ” she turns, and he follows.

## рҹҺ® Core mechanics

You are the wife (рҹ‘©) on a top-down canvas world. The husband (рҹ‘Ё) roams autonomously, driven by his own logic.

### The radius system

Every character has a radius вҖ” and they work in opposite ways:

- **Husband's radius** вҖ” the territory he explores. The wider it grows, the more of the world he opens up. He earns рҹӘҷ coins at work, and his radius grows with his income.
- **Wife's radius** вҖ” her zone of influence over him. The wife must stay within the husband's radius or the marriage begins to fall apart.
- **The trade-off:** when the wife spends coins on items, her radius of influence grows вҖ” but the husband's exploration radius shrinks. Every purchase is a negotiation between her power and his freedom.

### Tools of influence

| Action | What it does |
|--------|-------------|
| рҹ’Ӣ **Kiss** (Space) | Directs the husband toward a destination. Loses effectiveness if overused. Also the weapon used in boss fights |
| рҹӣӢпёҸ **Couch** | A trap вҖ” once the husband lands on it, he stops moving entirely |

> **Planned, not yet implemented:** рҹҳў Cry (draw him to you instantly), рҹ’¬ Talk (quiet his intrusive thoughts, reveal hidden interests)

### Resources

| Item | Effect |
|------|--------|
| рҹӘҷ Coins | Earned by the husband at work вҖ” his radius grows. The wife spends them on items |
| рҹҚ« Chocolate | Speed buff for the wife вҖ” essential during pregnancy |
| рҹ’„ Lipstick | Boosts the power of the kiss |

## рҹҺӯ The full lifecycle

The game simulates an **entire life:**

- Pregnancy, birth, children orbiting the wife вҖ” growing up and eventually leaving home
- Divorce is not a game over вҖ” a remarriage screen appears with new candidates, each procedurally generated
- Remarriage odds decrease with age, number of children, and previous divorces вҖ” based on real demographic data
- The game ends when the wife dies at a randomly determined age

Can you make it through a whole life?

## рҹҢҚ A social experiment (informal)

The author shared the game with a small number of people and noticed a striking pattern вҖ” though this is an informal observation, not a study:

Women who had ended multiple relationships tended to react with frustration: the mechanics felt unfair, the game felt punishing. Men вҖ” regardless of relationship history вҖ” tended to engage strategically. They gave tips. They admitted it was hard. They tried again.

Within each group, reactions were remarkably similar. Between the groups, they were worlds apart.

The reaction to the game became as interesting as the game itself.
